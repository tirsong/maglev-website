Research Overview

Socio-Technical Imaginaries of Maglev

This interactive report examines how magnetic levitation (maglev) technology is culturally translated across China, Japan, and Germany. It investigates how national media and public sentiment construct the concept of "frictionless travel"—framing it either as a site of ultimate modernity or a landscape of risk.

🚄

East Asia: The Sensory Upgrade

In China and Japan, the physical experience of levitation is actively marketed as a "sensory upgrade" to the human transit experience. The technology symbolizes national mastery, frictionless speed, and state-led or high-tech progress.

⚠️

Europe: Regulatory Caution

In Germany, following the 2006 Transrapid accident, public sentiment shifted from pioneering optimism to skepticism. The "levitating body" is viewed through a lens of regulatory caution and technological hubris.

Innovation Spotlight

Prof. Deng Zigang & HTS Maglev

At the Southwest Jiaotong University (SWJTU) in Chengdu, Professor Deng Zigang is pioneering the next frontier of transit: High-Temperature Superconducting (HTS) Maglev. Unlike traditional maglevs, HTS technology allows for "quantum pinning," where the vehicle remains locked in a stable levitation state even when stationary.

1

Super Maglev Prototype

The 2021 debut of the world's first high-temperature superconducting high-speed maglev engineering prototype, designed for speeds up to 620 km/h.

2

Vacuum Tube Integration

Deng's lab is researching the combination of HTS Maglev with low-vacuum tubes (the "Flying Train" concept) to potentially reach speeds exceeding 1,000 km/h.

🧪

SWJTU Super Maglev Lab

Established as the world's leading research center for HTS levitation stability and industrial application.
